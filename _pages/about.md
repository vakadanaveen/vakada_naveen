I am a Doctoral Researcher in the [HAIF (Human-Centric AI for Sustainable Future)](https://utu.fi/haif) program at the [University of Turku](https://www.utu.fi/en), affiliated with [ELLIS Finland](https://ellis.eu/units/turku) and TurkuNLP. I hold a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) Fellowship and am co-supervised by [Prof. Shaoxiong Ji](https://shaoxiong.fi/) (University of Turku) and [Prof. Desmond Elliott](https://elliottd.github.io/) (University of Copenhagen).

My research sits at the intersection of **multimodal deep learning**, **natural language processing**, and **audio-language modelling**. I am broadly interested in representation learning, reasoning, and real-world applications of generative AI — especially systems that understand the world through multiple modalities: text, vision, and audio.

Before joining Turku, I was a **Senior Machine Learning Researcher at Qualcomm R&D** (Hyderabad, 2024–2026), where I led projects on multimodal LLMs incorporating video, audio, and sensor data, and filed **8 patents** in audio and multimodal AI. Prior to that, I completed my **MS by Research in CSE at IIT Madras** (CGPA 9.38/10), working on visual paragraph generation and multimodal captioning under Prof. C. Chandra Sekhar.

I completed my **B.Tech. in CSE from VR Siddhartha Engineering College** (CGPA 9.58/10), where I received the Gold Medal for Best Outgoing Student. I also ranked **970 out of 100,000 candidates** in GATE CS 2020.

Outside of research, I enjoy board games, local food exploration, travel, and fitness technology.
